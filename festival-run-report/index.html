Festival Run Report

July 16–20, 2026 — new S32 digital-split rig, a week of gremlins, and a storm. A plain-English rundown of what broke and what fixed it.

1The big change — new rig

From an analog splitter snake to a single digital stage box feeding both desks.

The rig switched to a single S32 stage box split digitally over AES50 to both consoles:

  • FOH console → AES50 port A
  • Monitor console → AES50 port B

Two things this changed that matter going forward:

⚠️ Both desks now share the S32's preamps

There's only one preamp per channel, and whoever touches the gain last wins. The call: FOH owns gain — the monitor desk leaves preamps alone. Safe the preamp on the monitor desk so a scene recall can't grab it.

Also: the old "phantom power only on the direct leg" rule is gone — that was an analog-splitter thing. Phantom is just a normal check now.

⚠️ Clock: one master only

AES50 needs exactly one clock master. The right setup is FOH = Internal (master) → S32 slaves to port A → Monitor desk slaves to port B. Clock problems usually sound like clicks, ticks, or dropouts — not clean silence.

2Problems we chased, and what fixed each

Five faults, in the order they showed up.

  1. Mic barely showing signal even with gain maxed

    Turned out to be a bad cable at the stage box — not electronics. The thing that looks like every problem at once. Always worth ruling out early.

    Fixed: swapped the cable.

  2. Signal into the channel and the send, but nothing out of the Mix 1 wedge

    This was on the output side, not the desk. Seeing signal on the send only proves the channel feeds the bus — check whether the bus master meter actually moves to know if the bus itself is passing.

    Fixed on the output side.

  3. Mix bus 1 passed sound even with its fader all the way down

    The output tap point was set to pre-fader, so the fader was doing nothing. Set it back to post-fader and it behaved.

    Worth remembering: monitor buses want sends pre-fader but the output tap post-fader — two pre/post settings that want opposite answers. Easy to cross up while chasing a no-output problem.

    Fixed: tap point → post-fader.

  4. Mains went silent — LEDs and signal path looked fine

    Ruled out the DriveRack. Clean silence with good meters usually means it's downstream of the DSP — the output patch, or the stage box pulling its outputs from the wrong AES50 port. The show came back up.

    The exact cause never got logged — worth confirming next time.

  5. Clock setup was backwards for a while

    The monitor desk was on internal while FOH was slaving to AES50, leaving the chain without a clear single master. Corrected to FOH-as-master (see the rig section above).

    Fixed: FOH internal, both others slaved.

3Things worth keeping — how-tos

The stuff we set up that you'll want to do again.

Reverb

Feed it from a send bus (bus 13 → FX 1 by default), set that send post-fader, load the reverb on the FX page, bring up the FX return and assign it to the mains, then send channels to it. High-pass the return around 200–300 Hz to keep it from turning to mud.

Saving a scene

Save to an empty slot, name it something you'd recognize cold, and back it up to USB. Safes have nothing to do with saving — they only matter at recall, where they decide what a scene load is allowed to overwrite. Set them (Routing I/O, Output Patch, Preamp) before your first between-bands recall.

Proper shutdown

  1. Amps off firstAlways. Never power the desk down with amps live — that's how you thump the PA.
  2. Phantom off, then the restKill +48V, stop and eject any USB recording, confirm the scene saved, then consoles and S32 off.
  3. Power up in reverseEverything else first, amps last, once the network has settled.

Locking the surface

Setup → Global → Lock. It stops accidental bumps, but there's no password — it's a "don't touch," not real security.

4Quick answers

One-off questions that came up during the run.

  • iPad + Mix Station: wire the console to the router, get both the desk and iPad on the same network and subnet, connect. A "not in same subnet" error with matching IPs usually means a VPN or cellular path on the iPad — force-quitting the app cleared it.
  • Clip light stuck on but the meter looks fine: that's a latched peak indicator holding a past clip so you don't miss it. Clear it and watch — only act if it re-lights while someone's playing.
  • Router near the IEMs: the cable and data won't touch your audio. The only real concern is the router's Wi-Fi radio sitting inches from IEM antennas — give it a foot or two of separation (unless the IEMs are 2.4 GHz digital, then lock the router to 5 GHz).

5The storm — the serious one

Wet mains, a tripped GFCI, and a flash of white noise on transients.

The mains got wet in a storm and it tripped a GFCI. Afterward, vocal and drum transients caused a flash of white noise. We traced it to two bottom QSC line array boxes that got wet. The second box from the top on stage left had its Limit and Standby lights solid — its amp module had gone into protection — and it was arcing on transients (a flashover when peaks pushed current through where the water was).

🛑 A GFCI trip is a safety line, not just a gear problem

A trip means water bridged something live to ground. A powered box's amp module runs on line voltage internally, so a wet one that's arcing is a genuine shock/fire risk. That box got pulled from service — the right call.

Drying the wet boxes: they need to dry for days, in warm dry moving air, and ideally a tech's eyes before they see real load again. No heat gun, no "seems fine, repower it." Water plus a powered amp module is exactly the thing that fails after it dried wrong.

When boxes drop out of an array: don't crank the survivors to compensate — you just walk them into their own limiters and stress gear on a bad night. Reset system gain for the array you actually have.
